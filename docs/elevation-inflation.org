#+TITLE: Elevation gain inflation on GPS-denied (indoor) stretches
#+OPTIONS: toc:nil

* Summary

On a ~48 minute walk the *live* elevation-gain readout in the app climbed past
*500+ ft*, while the *saved GPX*, viewed afterward, looks legitimate — a flat
~20 m (66 ft) total range and only ~12 m of real climb. The inflation accrues
during *indoor / GPS-denied* stretches, where the barometer is the only
altitude source and nothing sanity-checks it.

This document records the observation and the leading hypothesis so we can
investigate properly once we have per-sample debug traces. *No fix has been
made; this is an investigation note, not a design.*

All coordinates in this document are *fabricated* — only the elevation/time
*pattern* is reproduced from the real recording.

* What was observed

- Trip: a ~48 minute walk (2026-06-12), recorded with a device that has a
  barometer.
- *During recording*, in the app: the elevation-gain stat climbed past ~500 ft.
- *After the walk*, viewing the saved =.gpx=: it looks fine — total elevation
  range ~20 m (66 ft).
- The walk included an *indoor portion*: GPS dropped out, the wearer kept
  moving and genuinely climbed about *3 storeys (~12 m)*, then GPS re-acquired
  in a different spot.

So the on-screen number and the file disagree by an order of magnitude, and the
real climb (~12 m) is a small fraction of what the app reported.

* The file and the live counter are two different numbers

They are fed by two *different sample streams*:

| Number             | Source                              | Cadence                          |
|--------------------+-------------------------------------+----------------------------------|
| Live gain (in app) | Barometer-driven fused altitude     | ~1 sample / 1–2 s, *continuously* |
| GPX =<ele>= points | Fused altitude at *accepted GPS fixes* | only when moving with a fix (5 m filter) |

Anything banked while GPS is unavailable has *no corresponding points in the
GPX*. The file therefore cannot show it, and a GPX viewer cannot recompute it.

What the recorded points imply for this walk:

| Method (on the saved points)               | Gain            |
|--------------------------------------------+-----------------|
| Total min→max elevation range              | 20 m (66 ft)    |
| Tracker-style 3 m hysteresis on the points | ~12 m (41 ft)   |
| Naive sum of every positive delta (noisy)  | ~101 m (331 ft) |
| *Live in-app counter (observed)*           | *500+ ft*       |

* The GPS gaps (anonymised)

The track split into 4 segments — i.e. 3 gaps where GPS was lost. The elevation
*change across each gap's endpoints* is modest; the real action happened
*inside* the gaps, unrecorded:

| Gap | Duration | Δ elevation across endpoints |
|-----+----------+------------------------------|
| 1→2 | ~133 s   | +8.2 m  (a real climb, e.g. going up)   |
| 2→3 | ~378 s   | −0.6 m                                  |
| 3→4 | ~600 s   | −0.4 m  (~10 min indoors; endpoints flat) |

The ~10 minute gap is the smoking gun: its endpoints are essentially level
(−0.4 m), yet the wearer was moving (and on stairs) the whole time, and the live
counter kept accruing — none of which is visible in the file.

* Why the barometer over-counts indoors

A barometer infers *relative* altitude from air pressure. Indoors that pressure
is hostile: HVAC pressurisation cycling, the stairwell "stack effect" in a
multi-storey building, doors opening, elevators, and temperature all produce
large *sustained* apparent altitude swings — not just the quick spikes the
smoothing is built to absorb.

The fusion + gain filter ([[../lib/elevation_tracker.dart][lib/elevation_tracker.dart]]) uses an exponential
moving average plus a *3 m sustained-climb hysteresis* with an 8-sample hold.
That is tuned for *outdoor* noise, and has two gaps that let indoor artifacts
through:

1. *No vertical climb-rate cap.* Nothing rejects an implausibly fast apparent
   climb. There is a horizontal "teleport" speed cap for distance (a fix
   implying impossible ground speed is rejected) but no elevation equivalent —
   a pressure jump implying a 10 m/s ascent is treated the same as real terrain.
2. *Once a climb is "confirmed", every new high accrues with no further
   threshold* (the =alt > _climbHigh= branch in =_update=). A slow upward drift
   or oscillation rides on top of the real climb and never ends — ending a climb
   requires a sustained 3 m *descent*.

With no GPS to anchor or bound it, indoor artifacts bank as climb: the real
~12 m plus an estimated ~140 m of artifact.

* What production apps do: DEM correction

Strava and Garmin do *not* trust the barometer alone for the final number. After
upload they apply server-side *Digital Elevation Model (DEM) correction*: the
track's GPS coordinates are sampled against a terrain-elevation database, and the
recorded elevations are replaced (or blended) with that known ground truth. The
elevation then comes from *where you were* (lat/lon → terrain height), not from
the pressure sensor, which sidesteps both barometric drift and the indoor
artifact problem.

Trade-off worth noting: a terrain DEM returns the *ground* height at a
coordinate, so it cannot see indoor floors — a 3-storey indoor climb would read
as ~0. Different failure mode, not strictly "better".

Err deliberately has *no server component* (privacy-first, offline, free), so
server-side DEM correction is out of scope. An on-device equivalent would mean
bundling or downloading terrain tiles.

- [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_elevation_model][Digital elevation model (Wikipedia)]] — the concept.
- [[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shuttle_Radar_Topography_Mission][SRTM]] — a widely used global elevation dataset.
- [[https://opentopography.org/][OpenTopography]] — open access to high-resolution topography / DEM data.
- [[https://www.usgs.gov/3d-elevation-program][USGS 3D Elevation Program (3DEP)]] — US national elevation data.

* Leading fix ideas (not implemented)

- *Vertical climb-rate cap.* Reject barometric gain that implies a faster ascent
  than a human climbs (~0.2–0.5 m/s) — the elevation analog of the existing
  horizontal teleport rejection. Keeps slow real climbs (stairs, hills), rejects
  fast pressure jumps (doors, HVAC, elevators).
- *Dead-band on "new high" accrual* so micro-oscillations during a confirmed
  climb don't each bank.

Both should be *validated against real per-sample data* before committing —
otherwise we are just moving the number around.

* How to gather better traces (debug mode)

The =.gpx= and summary =.csv= do *not* capture per-sample barometer values — the
flight recorder does. To investigate the next occurrence:

1. Turn it on: *Settings → Developer → Debug mode → on* (an "Open debug tools"
   row then appears).
2. With debug mode on, each recording also writes =<stamp>-debug.csv= next to the
   GPX — every raw GPS fix, every barometer sample, and the filter's verdict for
   each.
3. When elevation inflates again, keep that =-debug.csv=. It will show exactly
   which samples banked the phantom gain, and let us tune a fix against real
   numbers.

Note: debug mode was *off* for the 2026-06-12 walk, so only the GPX exists for
it — hence this is a hypothesis, not a confirmed root cause.
