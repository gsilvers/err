* Summary

Analysis of the real recorded track =2026-06-08T07-45-33.gpx= (Android /
GrapheneOS) against the code explains all three reported symptoms:

1. *UI showed ~1000 ft gain, GPX showed ~21 ft* — the UI and the GPX
   measured two unrelated data streams, and the UI's stream had a
   one-way-ratchet bug that banks every transient pressure spike forever.
2. *Phantom elevation cliff at the start* — the Android location stack
   switched providers mid-track, and the two providers report altitude in
   different reference frames ~33 m apart.
3. *Phantom distance jump* — same provider switch, plus an end-of-track
   cached fix ~218 m away from the previous point.

All three are fixed on branch =improve-accuracy= by routing every altitude
sample through a new, unit-tested =ElevationTracker= whose fused output
drives *both* the on-screen gain and the GPX =<ele>= values (so they agree
by construction), plus a teleport-rejection gate for distance.

** Two altitude reference frames

The points split cleanly into two populations:

| Population   | Timestamps           | Elevation range | Interpretation        |
|--------------+----------------------+-----------------+-----------------------|
| 1–7 and 21   | millisecond fraction | 130.5 – 138.7 m | provider A, MSL frame |
| 8–20         | whole seconds (.000) | 93.4 – 102.4 m  | provider B, WGS84 ellipsoid |

The offset between the populations is 32–45 m. The geoid undulation at
40.62°N, −75.36°W (eastern Pennsylvania) is ≈ −33 m, i.e. *exactly* the
difference between altitude above mean sea level and height above the WGS84
ellipsoid. This is not terrain and not noise — it is two Android location
sources disagreeing about what "altitude" means.

build before assuming a remaining bug. If it recurs, that is a background
execution problem, not an accuracy-filter problem.

* Root causes in the code (before the fix)

** RC1: UI gain and GPX elevations were unrelated data streams

- UI gain: accumulated in =_onBarometer= from the *barometric* sensor.
- GPX =<ele>=: raw =pos.altitude= from *GPS*.

There was no mechanism by which the two numbers could agree. Any
post-processing tool reading the GPX (which produced the realistic 21 ft)
computed from data the UI never used.

** RC2: barometer gain was a one-way ratchet on unsmoothed data

#+begin_src dart
// old code
final altDiff = alt - _lastBaroAltitude!;
if (altDiff > 3.0) {
  setState(() => _elevationGainMeters += altDiff);
}
_lastBaroAltitude = alt;
#+end_src

Two failure modes:

- *Overcount (the 1000 ft):* every transient pressure spike > 3 m — wind
  gust, pocket handling, a door closing — is banked permanently. The
  matching downward excursion never subtracts. Over 29 minutes at ~0.5 Hz,
  ~100 spikes × ~3 m ≈ 300 m ≈ 1000 ft of phantom gain. One-way thresholds
  on noisy signals *always* inflate.
- *Undercount:* a real, slow, steady climb that never moves 3 m between
  consecutive samples accumulates exactly *zero*. The GPS fallback (10 m
  per-sample threshold) had the same flaw.

** RC3: no defense against altitude reference-frame switches

The GPS fallback compared consecutive =pos.altitude= values directly, so an
upward MSL↔ellipsoid flip (+33 m in one step, with good claimed accuracy)
would be counted as instant climb.

** RC4: no teleport rejection for distance

=docs/gps-accuracy.md= already listed this as a known gap. A cached or
Wi-Fi fix far from the true position adds a phantom out-and-back spike.

* What Strava/Garmin do (and what is reproducible offline)

| Technique                              | Strava | Err now |
|----------------------------------------+--------+---------|
| Barometer for relative altitude        | yes    | yes     |
| GPS anchors barometer to absolute      | yes    | yes (frozen median offset) |
| Smoothing before gain accumulation     | yes    | yes (EMA, α = 0.3) |
| Minimum *sustained* gain (hysteresis)  | yes    | yes (3 m baro / 10 m GPS) |
| Server-side DEM terrain correction     | yes    | no — requires a server; out of scope for a local, privacy-first app |
| Implied-speed glitch rejection         | yes    | yes     |

* The fix

All on branch =improve-accuracy= (based on =origin/main= after PR #21).

** New: =lib/elevation_tracker.dart=

A pure-Dart, unit-testable class. Every altitude sample (baro and GPS) goes
through it; its fused output is the *single source of truth* for both the
UI gain and the GPX =<ele>=.

*** Fusion model
- Barometer provides relative change (~0.5 m resolution). Pressure →
  altitude via the international barometric formula.
- GPS anchors it to an absolute frame *once*: the offset is the median of
  the first 5 =(gps − baro)= differences, then *frozen* for the activity.
  A frozen offset means later provider frame-switches cannot bend the
  track; the worst case is a constant ~5–10 m absolute bias that does not
  affect gain at all.
- Baro samples are throttled to 1 Hz in code — the platform
  =samplingPeriod= is only a hint and some devices ignore it.

*** Gain algorithm (sustained-climb hysteresis)
1. Samples are EMA-smoothed (α = 0.3) so a one-sample spike reaches the
   accumulator at ~30 % of its size, below threshold.
2. While *not climbing*, a running local minimum (the floor) follows every
   descent for free.
3. A climb is *confirmed* only when smoothed altitude rises a full
   threshold above the floor — 3 m (baro) or 10 m (GPS) — and the rise is
   banked.
4. While *climbing*, every new high accrues continuously, so slow steady
   ascents are captured in full (the naive per-sample threshold lost
   them entirely).
5. A threshold-sized descent ends the climb.

Symmetric noise never confirms a climb → flat terrain reads ~0.

*** GPS-fallback guards (no barometer)
- Fixes with =altitudeAccuracy ≥ 15 m= excluded.
- A jump > 25 m between consecutive fixes is treated as a reference-frame
  switch: the tracker *rebases* to the new altitude, counting nothing.

** Changed: =lib/main.dart=
- =_segments= now stores =_TrackPoint= (position + fused elevation at
  record time); =_saveGpx= writes the fused elevation. *UI and GPX now
  agree by construction.*
- Teleport rejection: a fix whose implied speed exceeds
  =max(3 × pos.speed, 15 m/s)= is rejected (Doppler-derived =pos.speed= is
  far more trustworthy than position deltas). Three consecutive rejections
  mean the previous anchor was the glitch → re-anchor in a new GPX segment,
  counting no distance. Rejected points are also excluded from the GPX, so
  file-derived distance matches the UI too.
- Old =_onBarometer= / =_elevationGainMeters= ratchet logic deleted.

Rewritten §4–§6, pipeline summary, and limitations to match the new

* Remaining limitations (accepted trade-offs)

- *Barometric weather drift* — ~1 hPa/hr storm front ≈ 8 m/hr apparent
  drift. The hysteresis absorbs most of it; full elimination needs a DEM
  server, which conflicts with the local-only philosophy.
- *Micro-terrain below threshold* — hills under 3 m (baro) / 10 m (GPS)
  of relief record no gain. Strava/Garmin flatten the same terrain.
- *Frozen calibration* — absolute GPX elevations can carry the initial GPS
  vertical error (~5–10 m) as a constant bias; relative changes and gain
  are unaffected.
- *Distance accuracy gate (25 m) can starve the track* — in poor signal
  (phone in pocket, tree cover) every fix may exceed 25 m and the track
  pauses. Conservative and Strava-like, but if field tests show long
  starved stretches, an adaptive gate (accept up to ~50 m after long
  starvation) is the next candidate improvement.

* Live debugging facilities (implemented — PR #23, stacked on PR #22)

Field testing needs visibility into *filter decisions*, not just outputs.
The key unanswered question from the 2026-06-08 track — during the
26-minute silence, did the OS deliver no fixes at all, or did the 25 m
accuracy gate reject everything? — is exactly the kind of question a debug
facility must be able to answer.

** Architecture: one diagnostics core, three consumers

#+begin_example
_onPosition / _onBarometer / ElevationTracker
        │  (every raw sample + every filter verdict)
        ▼
TrackingDiagnostics (ChangeNotifier)
  ├─ ring buffer: last ~500 events
  ├─ counters: accept / rej-acc / rej-stale / rej-teleport / splits
  ├─ latest raw fix, latest baro, tracker snapshot
  │
  ├──▶ Log tab        (live scrolling stream, filter chips, autoscroll)
  ├──▶ REPL tab       (mini-lisp, read-only builtins over the same state)
  └──▶ flight recorder (<stamp>-debug.csv next to the GPX)
#+end_example

Every raw fix is logged *with its verdict* (=ACCEPT=, =REJECT acc>25
(41.3m)=, =STALE=, =TELEPORT 2/3 implied=38m/s cap=15=, =SEGMENT-SPLIT
gap=1553s=), turning "distance looks wrong" into "47 fixes rejected by the
accuracy gate between 11:48 and 12:14".

** Activation — hidden, non-default

Long-press the "Err" app-bar title toggles debug mode (persisted in
prefs). A bug icon then appears, opening a full-screen debug route with
two tabs: *Log* and *REPL*. Runtime toggle rather than =kDebugMode=
gating, because field tests run sideloaded *release* builds. Keeps the
default UI clean per the simplicity philosophy.

** Log tab

Live scrolling stream of the diagnostics ring buffer, newest at bottom,
with autoscroll pause and filter chips (GPS / BARO / ELEV / verdicts).

** REPL tab — a minimal lisp over read-only app state

A ~300-line hand-written lisp interpreter in pure Dart (no new
dependencies): numbers, strings, symbols, lists; =quote= =if= =define=
=lambda= =let=; =car/cdr/cons/list/map/filter/assoc/length= plus
arithmetic and comparison. App state is exposed as read-only builtins
returning assoc lists, so queries compose:

#+begin_src lisp
err> (stats)
((distance . 1243.7) (gain . 12.4) (elapsed . 754) (points . 213) (segments . 1))

err> (gps)                          ; latest RAW fix, pre-filter
((lat . 40.62277) (lon . -75.36254) (acc . 8.2) (alt . 98.4)
 (alt-acc . 11.0) (speed . 1.38) (age . 2.1) (ms-frac . t))

err> (elev)                         ; ElevationTracker internals
((fused . 99.1) (floor . 97.8) (climbing . nil) (gain . 12.4)
 (baro . t) (offset . 47.3) (cal-n . 5) (rebases . 0))

err> (counters)
((accept . 213) (rej-acc . 47) (rej-stale . 1) (rej-teleport . 2) (splits . 1))

err> ;; how bad was accuracy over the last 50 raw fixes?
err> (map (lambda (e) (cdr (assoc 'acc e))) (gps-history 50))
(8.2 7.9 31.4 8.8 ...)

err> (length (filter (lambda (e) (> (cdr (assoc 'acc e)) 25))
             (gps-history 50)))
12                                  ; ← the accuracy gate's victims, live
#+end_src

Notes:
- =ms-frac= reports whether the fix timestamp carries a millisecond
  fraction — the fingerprint that distinguished the two location
  providers in the 2026-06-08 GPX. The provider switch becomes visible
  live.
- The log is queryable from the REPL too (=(log 20)=, =(log 20
  'reject)=) — the tabs are views over the same data, not separate
  systems.
- Phone-keyboard ergonomics: a quick-insert row above the input
  (=( ) ' lambda assoc=) plus up-arrow command history.
- v1 is strictly read-only — every builtin is a getter. Runtime tuning
  (e.g. =(set-gate 'accuracy 50)= for field experiments) is a natural
  later step but introduces mutation and deserves its own decision.

** Flight recorder

When debug mode is on, each trip also writes =<stamp>-debug.csv= beside
the GPX: every raw fix (pre-filter) + verdict + tracker-state snapshot,
and throttled baro samples. Buffered appends; negligible battery. This is
the higher-value half for field testing — the live screens only help
while looking at the phone, and the worst observed failure (the 26-minute
silence) happened in a pocket. Post-walk, the debug CSV lines up against
the GPX row by row.
