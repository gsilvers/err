* Findings in detail

** F1 — Start-of-track cliff regression (135.93 → 84.43 m)

GPX points 1–5 (=<ele>= 135.93, 84.43, 84.43, 85.43, 86.43) are raw GPS
altitudes because fused output doesn't exist until =BARO calibrated= at
21:39:33 (needs 5 fixes). Point 1 is the regime-B anchor; points 2–5 are
regime A. The cross-frame cliff that =improve-accuracy= fixed for the
track body survives in the pre-calibration window.

Possible fixes: buffer the first segment's points and backfill =<ele>=
once calibration completes; or withhold =<ele>= until fused exists; or
seed calibration from the anchor fix immediately and refine.

** F2 — Calibration locked onto the biased provider

=BARO calibrated — offset −41.7 m (median of 5 fixes)= → raw 127.5 m
mapped to ~85.8 m. The five fixes were all regime A (median 85.4 m),
whose tight alt_acc (1–3 m) is unearned: the same provider drifted +5 m
over 40 s on flat ground and sits ~37 m off the other frame. Every fused
altitude and GPX =<ele>= for the rest of the walk inherits the −40 m
bias. When regime B arrived at 21:55:46 reporting 130.7 m against fused
90.1 m, nothing re-examined the offset.

Possible fixes: detect a step discontinuity between GPS altitude and
fused (e.g. sustained >20 m residual) and re-calibrate; weight
calibration by something other than the provider's self-reported
alt_acc; prefer fixes whose fingerprint (fractional timestamps,
continuous accuracy) marks them as raw GNSS.

** F3 — Phantom gain on flat ground (~10 of 22.7 m)

Baro raw was 127 ± 1 m from 21:37 to ~21:53 — flat. Yet banked gain
reached 9.1 m by 21:54:50 (climbs confirmed at 21:40:14 and 21:50:55).

/Correction (found while implementing the fixes):/ the original draft
blamed GPS-altitude wiggle tugging the fused stream, but =addGps= is
already ignored once the barometer is active — fused shape is pure baro.
The actual mechanism: the smoothed baro wobbles ±1.5 m around a slowly
drifting baseline while the floor ratchets down to every local minimum,
so the 3.0 m confirm threshold gets crossed occasionally on flat ground
— and each confirmation banks the full floor-to-peak excursion. The
climb-end churn (F4) then compounds it by resetting the floor into noise
dips. Mitigation implemented: confirmations and ends must now hold for
several consecutive samples (transient excursions never bank); slow
genuine climbs still accrue in full at confirmation. Residual risk:
sustained multi-minute pressure drift within the threshold band can
still bank small phantom climbs — acceptable trade-off vs missing real
one-storey climbs.

** F4 — Climb end/re-confirm churn double-counts

At 21:54:39–21:54:51 fused dipped 89.07 → 86.98 → 85.18 (GPS accepts
pulling down) while baro raw was *rising* (129 m, real climb starting).
The dip triggered =CLIMB ended= (floor reset to 85.18); 2.5 s later
=CLIMB confirmed= re-banked 3.1 m from the artificially lowered floor.
3 m of vertical in 2.5 s is not a walking human. Add a time/altitude
hysteresis on climb end, or set the ended-climb floor to a recent median
rather than the instantaneous fused value.
